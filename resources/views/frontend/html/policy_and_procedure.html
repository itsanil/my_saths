4.24 - Telemarketing

The Federal Trade Commission and the Federal Communications Commission each have laws that restrict telemarketing practices.
Both federal agencies (as well as a number of states) have "do not call" regulations as part of their telemarketing laws. While you may not consider yourself a "telemarketer" in the traditional sense of the word, these regulations broadly define the term "telemarketer" and "telemarketing" so that your inadvertent action of calling someone whose telephone number is listed on the federal "do not call" registry could cause you to violate the law. Moreover, these regulations must not be taken lightly, as they carry significant penalties (up to $11,000.00 per violation).
Therefore, Managers must not engage in telemarketing relative to the operation of their  Saath ecommerce pvt ltd's Mysaathe website   projects. The term "telemarketing" means the placing of one or more telephone calls to an individual or entity to induce the purchase of a  Saath ecommerce pvt ltd's Mysaathe website   product or service, or to recruit them for the  Saath ecommerce pvt ltd's Mysaathe website   platform. However, a telephone call(s) placed to a prospective customer or Manager (a "prospect") is permissible under the following situations:

  • If the Manager has an established business relationship with the prospect. An "established business relationship" is a relationship between a Manager and a prospect based on the prospect's purchase, rental or lease of goods or services from the Manager, or a financial transaction between the prospect and the Manager, within the 18 months immediately preceding the date of a telephone call to induce the prospect's purchase of a product or service.
  • The prospect's personal inquiry or application regarding a product or service offered by the Manager within the 3 months immediately preceding the date of such a call.
  • If the Manager receives written and signed permission from the prospect authorizing the Manager to call. The authorization must specify the telephone number(s) that the Manager is authorized to call.
  • You may call family members, personal friends and acquaintances. An "acquaintance" is someone with whom you have at least a recent first-hand relationship (i.e., you have recently personally met him or her). Bear in mind, however, that if you make a habit of "card collecting" with everyone you meet and subsequently calling them, the FTC may consider this a form of telemarketing that is not subject to this exemption.
